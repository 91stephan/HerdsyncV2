  "Digitalisation of National Breeding Centers" under WaMCoP
CONTRACTOR: [Your Company Name] — acting as technology subcontractor
PRIME CONTRACTOR: CBS (lead firm under JV arrangement)
CONTRACT TYPE: Quality and Cost-Based Selection (QCBS)

BACKGROUND:
The Kingdom of Lesotho's economy is substantially dependent on wool
and mohair production from Merino sheep and Angora goats. The
WaMCoP (Wool and Mohair Competitiveness Project) project, funded
by the International Fund for Agricultural Development (IFAD),
mandates the digitalisation of national breeding centers to:
- Prevent inbreeding in the national stud through pedigree tracking
- Enable the Ministry to calculate and report Basotho livestock
  indices (mortality rate, offtake rate, lambing/kidding rate)
- Support the Ethical Fashion Initiative (EFI) Basotho Brand
  certification for international luxury wool/mohair markets
- Achieve WOAH/OIE disease notification compliance
- Connect rural extension workers in Quthing and Mokhotlong
  districts via offline-capable mobile tools

BREEDING CENTERS IN SCOPE:
- Semonkong Merino Stud Center (Maseru District)
- Mohale's Hoek Angora Breeding Center
- Quthing National Stud Center
- Mokhotlong Highland Breeding Center
- Leribe Fiber Production Center

SYSTEM BEING DELIVERED:
A self-hosted React/TypeScript web and mobile application backed by
a self-hosted PostgreSQL (Supabase) stack deployed on Lesotho
Government infrastructure. Key modules:
1. National livestock registry (ISO 11784/11785 RFID, 15-digit ID)
2. Pedigree lineage engine (multi-generational, inbreeding coefficient)
3. Fiber production tracking (fleece weight, micron, staple, yield)
4. Culling & Exchange program workflow
5. WOAH/OIE-compliant disease reporting
6. Genetic indices calculator (Basotho-specific)
7. Offline field sync for remote districts
8. LIAMIS and WaMCoP MIS integration
9. EFI fiber data exports
